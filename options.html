About Look Away Reminder

Look Away Reminder is a simple Chrome extension that gently reminds you to look away from your screen at regular intervals. Spending long periods focused on a screen can feel uncomfortable for many people. This extension supports a common screen-break habit by prompting you to briefly shift your gaze away from your display. It runs quietly in the background and does not track your activity.

No accounts. No analytics. No distractions.

How it works

  • Sends a reminder at regular intervals
  • Encourages you to look away from your screen for a short break
  • Plays an optional soft sound so the reminder is not missed
  • Uses Chrome’s built-in notifications
  • Works even when the popup is closed

The goal is simple: help you remember to pause and rest your eyes during long screen sessions.

Why people use it

Supports screen break habits

Many people forget to take breaks while working on a computer. A gentle reminder can help reinforce healthier screen habits without interrupting work.

Encourages distance viewing

Looking away from close screens and focusing on distant objects allows the eyes to change focus, which many users find more comfortable than continuous near viewing.

Helps counter reduced blinking

Research shows that people tend to blink less while looking at screens. Reminders act as a cue to relax and blink naturally again.

Lightweight and private

The extension does not read page content, track browsing, or collect personal data. It only runs a timer and shows reminders.

Scientific background (plain-language summary)

This extension is inspired by well-documented observations in vision and occupational health research:

  • Screen use reduces blink rate: Studies show that sustained screen attention often lowers natural blink frequency, which may contribute to eye dryness and discomfort.
  • Extended near-focus can cause visual fatigue: Prolonged close-up focusing places continuous demand on the eye’s focusing system. Shifting gaze to distant objects allows that system to relax.
  • Short, regular breaks are commonly recommended: Vision and occupational health organizations often recommend frequent short breaks rather than infrequent long ones.

The commonly cited “20-20-20” idea is a guideline, not a medical rule. The extension supports the general concept of regular visual breaks, not a specific medical treatment.

What this extension does NOT do

  • It does not diagnose or treat medical conditions
  • It does not claim to prevent or cure eye problems
  • It does not replace professional eye care
  • It does not monitor health metrics

This extension is a reminder tool, not a medical device.
